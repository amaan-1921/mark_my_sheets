1. What is the capital of France?
[10 marks]
The capital of France is Paris. Paris is the largest city in France and serves as its political, economic, and cultural center. It is located in northern France on the Seine River.

3. What are the three branches of government?
[10 marks]
The three branches of government are the executive branch (headed by the President), the legislative branch (Congress, including the House of Representatives and Senate), and the judicial branch (the Supreme Court and other federal courts). This separation of powers ensures checks and balances in the government system.

4. Define Newton's First Law of Motion.
[10 marks]
Newton's First Law of Motion, also known as the Law of Inertia, states that an object at rest stays at rest and an object in motion stays in motion with the same speed and in the same direction unless acted upon by an unbalanced force.

5. What is the significance of the year 1492?
[5 marks]
The year 1492 is significant because it marks when Christopher Columbus first voyaged to the Americas, leading to European exploration and colonization of the New World.
